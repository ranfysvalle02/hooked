Structural equivalence

          

            What you just watched is RLHF — pointed at engagement instead of alignment.
          

          

            The gradient that creates the outrage spiral and the gradient that aligns
            ChatGPT are the same gradient. The only thing that changes between a
            feed that destroys you and a model that helps you is the scalar reward
            $R_t$ you plug in.
          


          

            

              
LLM pretraining

              
the prior

              
$\mathcal{L} \;=\; -\log P(\text{next token})$

              

                Corpus frequency, exponentially sharpened by softmax. A logit gap of 2.0 becomes
                7.4× probability. This is why the model “recommends” Postgres.
              

            


            

              
Engagement maximization

              
the trap

              
$R_t \;=\; G_t$

              

                Raw human reaction. No constraint. The math finds outrage because outrage
                produces the highest variance returns — exactly what you saw.
              

            


            

              
RLHF / alignment

              
the “cure”

              

                $R_t \;=\; r_\phi(s_t,a_t) \,-\, \beta\,\mathrm{KL}\!\big(\pi_\theta \,\|\, \pi_{\text{ref}}\big)$
              

              

                Learned preference reward minus a KL leash to a reference policy.
                This is the literal update used to train ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.
              

            


            

              
This demo, with $\lambda>0$

              
the slider above

              
$R_t \;=\; G_t \,-\, \lambda\,\rho(s_t,a_t)$

              

                Engagement reward minus an arousal penalty. Structurally identical to RLHF:
                a constraint subtracted from a raw preference signal. $\lambda$ is the twin of $\beta$.
              

            

          


          

            
All four. Same exponential amplifier. Different scalar.

            

              $\theta \;\leftarrow\; \theta \,+\, \alpha\, \nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta(a_t \mid s_t) \,\cdot\, R_t$
            

            

              $G_t$↔$r_\phi$
              $\lambda\,\rho$↔$\beta\,\mathrm{KL}$
              $-\log P$↔softmax
              preference signal ↔ soft constraint ↔ exponential sharpening
            

          


          

            LLM pretraining, engagement maximization, RLHF alignment, and this demo
            are the same math pointed in four different directions. The model that
            “recommends” Postgres, the feed that hooks you on outrage, and
            the chatbot that politely helps you — all one gradient, all one exponential
            amplifier. Whoever defines $R_t$ is the actual sovereign
            — the gradient is indifferent.

          About this model
          

            This demonstration uses a 3-state Markov chain (calm → engaged → hooked)
            to model user arousal escalation. Real recommender systems operate over continuous
            embedding spaces with millions of latent user states. The simplification preserves
            the core mechanism: when rewards are state-dependent and high-arousal states amplify
            engagement signals, gradient ascent on engagement creates a self-reinforcing trap.
            The insight generalizes to any system where (1) the user's internal state affects
            reward magnitude, (2) the system's actions influence state transitions, and
            (3) optimization pressure favors actions that escalate state. These three conditions
            hold in every major attention-economy platform today.
          

          

            The RLHF connection. The well-being objective in this demo
            ($R_t = G_t - \lambda\,\rho(s,a)$) is a stripped-down RLHF reward
            ($R_t = r_\phi - \beta\,\mathrm{KL}$). Both subtract a soft constraint from a
            scalar preference signal and feed the result into the same REINFORCE update.
            $\lambda$ in this simulator plays the structural role of $\beta$ in RLHF,
            and $\rho(s,a)$ plays the role of the KL divergence to a reference policy.
            The arousal penalty stops the policy from drifting into outrage; the KL penalty
            stops a fine-tuned LLM from drifting away from its base distribution. Same
            mechanism, different reference point.
